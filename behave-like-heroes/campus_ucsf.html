Section A — Department Overview
Agency: University of California San Francisco Police Department (UCSF PD)  |  Current Chief: Mike Denson (July 2015–present)  |  Sworn Officers: 58 (2019 UC Presidential Task Force — current staffing not independently verified as of March 2026)  |  Annual Budget: $50,000,000+ (largest in UC system)  |  Accreditation: CALEA Advanced Level (2008) + IACLEA (2025) — only UC PD holding both
Key Distinctions — UCSF PD Within the UC System UCSF is the only UC campus dedicated exclusively to the health sciences, with no undergraduate students. This unique mission produces a policing environment and a regulatory burden without parallel at any other UC campus. The distinctions documented below establish the factual context required to evaluate UCSF PD's compliance posture against systemwide norms.
FieldDetail
Agency NameUniversity of California San Francisco Police Department (UCSF PD)
Campus ProfileUCSF is the only UC campus dedicated exclusively to the health sciences. The campus has no undergraduate students. Four postgraduate professional schools — dentistry, pharmacy, nursing, and medicine — enroll approximately 3,100 students. The institution employs tens of thousands of faculty, staff, researchers, and clinicians.
Legal AuthorityCalifornia Penal Code §830.2(a) (sworn peace officers pursuant to Education Code §92600). Full statewide law enforcement authority. Additionally subject to hospital-specific security mandates: Health & Safety Code §§1257.7–1257.8 (AB 508, 1993), Cal/OSHA §3342, Labor Code §6401.8 (SB 1299, 2014), and SB 553 (2023). This dual regulatory framework — standard UC police mandates plus hospital security statutes — is unique within the UC system.
POST StatusPOST-participating agency. All officers hold POST certification. Subject to SB 2 (Kenneth Ross Jr. Act) certification and decertification authority, effective January 1, 2023.
Sworn Officers58 sworn police officers as reported in the 2019 UC Presidential Task Force Report — third-largest in the UC system at that time, behind UCLA (64) and UC Berkeley (56). Current sworn staffing not independently verified as of March 2026; no more recent sworn officer count has been identified in publicly accessible UCSF PD documents. Total FTE including sworn and non-sworn combined (including medical center security): approximately 440 employees — as reported in Chief Denson's professional profile and UCSF Finance and Administrative Services records; current figure not independently verified against a publicly accessible primary document as of March 2026. This total represents the largest total public safety workforce in the UC system. Institutional context: UCSF's total campus-wide workforce has grown to more than 36,000 faculty, staff, and clinicians as of 2024, as confirmed by Chancellor Hawgood's 2024 State of the University address.
Annual BudgetExceeding $50,000,000 — as reported in Chief Denson's professional profile and UCSF Finance and Administrative Services records; an independently confirmed UCSF PD-specific budget document has not been identified in available public records as of March 2026. This figure represents the largest departmental police budget in the UC system. The scale reflects the medical center security mission, multi-site geographic footprint across more than 230 UCSF-operated locations in San Francisco and the Bay Area, and 24-hour hospital security obligations. A publicly accessible UC systemwide Community Safety Budget and Workforce Dashboard is maintained by UCOP at: ucop.edu — Community Safety Data Dashboard.
Officer-to-Student Ratio18.58 officers per 1,000 students — by far the highest ratio in the UC system. Next highest: UC Merced 2.13, UCSB 1.84, UCR 1.50, UCB 1.48, UCLA 1.41. This ratio reflects the medical center mission: officers protect hospitals, research facilities, patient populations, and visitor traffic — not a traditional residential student campus.
Geographic JurisdictionGeographically dispersed across San Francisco and beyond: Parnassus Heights Campus; Mission Bay Campus; Mount Zion Campus; UCSF Benioff Children's Hospital Oakland; China Basin Campus; San Francisco General Hospital; SF Veterans Affairs Medical Center; UC College of the Law (contract); and over 100 additional properties in San Francisco, Daly City, and South San Francisco. The UCSF employment page confirms the institution operates at more than 230 sites in San Francisco, representing the total institutional footprint within which UCSF PD maintains law enforcement jurisdiction. Source: UCSF PD Employment page — police.ucsf.edu/join-our-team-employment.
Current ChiefMike Denson (July 2015–present, approximately 10 years including interim period). Previously: 21 years with the Palo Alto Police Department, retiring as Captain (assignments included criminal investigations, patrol, personnel/training, internal affairs, hostage negotiations, and SWAT). Deputy Chief of Police, San Mateo County Transit District (88-mile rail/bus system). Joined UCSF PD in 2012 as Captain, Field Services Division — second in command to Chief Roskowski. B.S., Public Administration (University of San Francisco); M.S., Emergency Services Administration (CSU Long Beach). Graduate of the FBI National Academy (Quantico, Virginia).
AccreditationCALEA (Commission on Accreditation for Law Enforcement Agencies) — Advanced Level — achieved 2008. The only UC police department to have ever obtained CALEA accreditation. CALEA requires compliance with more than 480 professional standards. Accreditation is renewed every four years. One of approximately 60 university police forces worldwide to hold CALEA recognition. Additionally holds IACLEA accreditation per UC Board of Regents C4 document, January 22, 2025. The only UC police department holding both CALEA and IACLEA accreditation simultaneously.
Oversight BodyPolice Accountability Board (PAB) — reviews complaint investigation reports, makes recommendations to the Chief. 2024 annual report published and publicly accessible. Complaint process map and investigation timeline publicly available. Any person directly affected by officer conduct may file a complaint. Website: pab.ucsf.edu.
Union RepresentationFederated University Police Officers' Association (FUPOA). Prior to the FUPOA's systemwide formation, UCSF had an independent Police Officers Association at the time of Chief Roskowski's 2003 appointment, with Scott Andalon serving as president.
Organizational StructureFive divisions reporting to Chief Denson: (1) Office of the Chief; (2) Field Services Division — patrol and investigations from Parnassus and Mission Bay sub-stations; (3) Professional Standards Division — internal affairs, recruiting, training, and CALEA compliance; (4) Security Services Division — medical center security operations; (5) Technical Services Division — emergency communications center, LiveScan fingerprinting, property and evidence management.
Notable Distinctions(1) Only UC PD with CALEA accreditation (Advanced Level, 2008–present). (2) Most stable chief leadership in the UC system: only 2 chiefs in 22+ years. (3) Only documented line-of-duty death in UC police system history: Detective Rex Veloro, October 22, 2009. (4) Only UC PD operating under hospital-specific security mandates (HSC §§1257.7–1257.8; Cal/OSHA §3342; SB 553). (5) Only UC campus under active federal congressional investigation for antisemitism with UCSF PD document production obligations (2024–present). (6) Largest total public safety workforce and largest budget in the UC system. (7) Highest officer-to-student ratio in the UC system (18.58/1,000).
Section B — Chronological Incident Log

All incidents documented below are drawn from public records, independent journalistic investigations, court records, federal government publications, and official university statements. They are presented in chronological order without editorial characterization. 2003 — Chief Appointment
CHIEF ROSKOWSKI APPOINTMENT — PRIOR AGENCY CONTROVERSY AND HIRING CONTEXT

UCSF hired Pamela Roskowski from the Corvallis, Oregon Police Department, where she had served as chief for approximately 12 years (c. 1990–2003). Prior to her hiring by UCSF, the following documented events had occurred at her prior agency:

April 2002 — No-Confidence Vote: At a union meeting held in April 2002, 94% of officers present voted "no confidence" in Chief Roskowski's leadership at the Corvallis Police Department. Documented allegations associated with the no-confidence proceedings included: expenditure of funds on consultants whose recommendations were not implemented; a $500,000 records management system project reported as a failure; increased rates of stress-related illness and turnover among officers; and short-staffed patrol operations. A prior no-confidence vote had also been considered in April 1995.

2001 — Oregon DOJ Investigation: Separate from the no-confidence proceedings, a 2001 allegation was made that Roskowski had ordered officers to dismiss a minor-in-possession citation issued to a 20-year-old individual who was the child of a personal acquaintance. The Oregon Department of Justice investigated this allegation and found no criminal wrongdoing.

UCSF Hiring Process: At the time of her UCSF hiring, the UCSF Police Officers Association president, Scott Andalon, attempted to block the appointment. Andalon wrote to UCSF Associate Vice Chancellor Randy Lopez that "several serious issues regarding Pam Roskowski's tenure at the Corvallis Police Department have been brought to my attention." Associate Vice Chancellor Lopez responded that Roskowski was "clearly a consensus first choice" of the search process.

2003 — Defamation Lawsuit: Following her departure from Corvallis, Roskowski filed a $6 million defamation lawsuit against the Corvallis Police Officers Association and Teamsters Local 223 (Roskowski v. Corvallis POA, filed 2003). The United States District Court dismissed the lawsuit. The United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit affirmed the dismissal in 2007 under the Communications Decency Act (CDA), 47 U.S.C. §230.

Applicable Standards: POST standards governing chief-level hiring and vetting; institutional integrity obligations. The Oregon DOJ cleared the criminal allegation. The federal lawsuit was dismissed by both the District Court and the Ninth Circuit. This entry is included to document the factual hiring context only.

Reported to POST/DOJ? Not applicable — pre-appointment controversy at prior agency. Oregon DOJ cleared criminal allegation. Federal lawsuit dismissed with prejudice.

Sources: Gazette-Times (Corvallis), December 30, 2002; Democrat-Herald (Albany, OR), December 15, 2022; Online Liability Blog, November 29, 2007; Blue Moon Consulting Group professional biography.
2008
CALEA ACCREDITATION ACHIEVED — FIRST AND ONLY IN UC SYSTEM

In 2008, the UCSF Police Department achieved accreditation from the Commission on Accreditation for Law Enforcement Agencies (CALEA). UCSF PD became, and remains, the only UC police department to have ever attained CALEA accreditation. CALEA requires agencies to demonstrate compliance with more than 480 professional law enforcement standards. The UCSF PD subsequently achieved CALEA's Advanced Level accreditation. CALEA accreditation is renewed through a rigorous re-assessment process every four years. UCSF PD is one of approximately 60 university police forces worldwide to hold CALEA recognition.

No other UC campus police department has pursued CALEA accreditation. As of January 22, 2025, the UC Board of Regents C4 document additionally records UCSF PD as holding IACLEA accreditation — making UCSF PD the only UC police department to hold both CALEA and IACLEA accreditation simultaneously.

Applicable Standards: CALEA accreditation standards (480+ professional standards). Voluntary external accountability framework exceeding requirements at all other UC campuses.

Reported to POST/DOJ? Not applicable — accreditation milestone.

Sources: UCSF PD website — police.ucsf.edu; UCOP, January 22, 2013; UC Board of Regents C4 document, January 22, 2025 — regents.universityofcalifornia.edu/regmeet/jan25/c4.pdf.
October 22, 2009
LINE-OF-DUTY DEATH — DETECTIVE REX VELORO — ONLY DOCUMENTED LODD IN UC POLICE SYSTEM HISTORY

On October 22, 2009, UCSF Police Detective Rex Veloro was killed in an automobile accident in the line of duty. Detective Veloro had joined the UCSF Police Department as a police officer in October 2002, following two years of service with the San Francisco County Sheriff's Office. He had been designated the 2008 UCSF Police Department Employee of the Year.

In July 2004, Detective Veloro received the UC Police Department Meritorious Service Medal for, in the department's documentation, "exercising exceptional judgment, restraint, and professionalism while apprehending a subject armed with a knife."

UCSF Police Dispatcher John Dragon was critically injured in the same accident and was admitted to San Francisco General Hospital in critical condition.

Chief Roskowski issued a public statement: "Officer Veloro was an outstanding, well-respected member of the UCSF Police Department. This is a tragic loss for us as a department and as professional colleagues and friends."

This event is the only documented line-of-duty death in the entire history of the University of California police system across all ten campuses.

Applicable Standards: POST line-of-duty death protocols; Peace Officers Memorial Foundation reporting requirements; Clery Act (20 U.S.C. §1092(f)) incident notification obligations.

Reported to POST/DOJ? Documented — this line-of-duty death is recorded in institutional records. Included as the sole documented LODD in UC police system history.

Sources: UCSF News, October 23, 2009 — "UCSF Mourns Campus Police Detective".
2011 — Post-Pepper Spray Period
SYSTEM-WIDE POLICY REFORM COORDINATION — CHIEF ROSKOWSKI AS UC SYSTEM-WIDE POLICE SERVICES COORDINATOR

Following the UC Davis pepper spray incident of November 18, 2011, and the UC Berkeley Occupy Cal incident of November 2011, the UC system undertook a system-wide review and reform of use-of-force and civil disobedience response policies. Chief Roskowski was serving at this time as the UC System-wide Police Services Coordinator — a role in which she oversaw coordination, development, and implementation of public safety planning and policy across all ten UC campuses.

In her capacity as System-wide Coordinator — a role she held for six years — Roskowski was instrumental in developing and implementing the revised system-wide Use of Force policy and the Civil Disobedience Response policy adopted by the UC system in the aftermath of the 2011 incidents. These policies applied to all ten UC police departments. This coordination role placed a UCSF-based chief at the center of the UC-wide reform process.

Applicable Standards: UC system-wide use-of-force policy development; POST training standards; system-wide civil disobedience response protocols.

Reported to POST/DOJ? Not applicable — policy reform coordination role.

Sources: Blue Moon Consulting Group professional biography of Chief Roskowski; UCOP records.
March 2024
UCSF CANCER CENTER DEFACED WITH ANTISEMITIC LANGUAGE INVOKING THE HOLOCAUST

In March 2024, UCSF's cancer center was defaced with antisemitic language that invoked the Holocaust. This incident was subsequently cited by the United States House Energy and Commerce Committee in its July 2024 investigation correspondence as evidence of an antisemitic environment at UCSF facilities. It was referenced alongside other documented incidents in the committee's seven-page letter to UCSF Chancellor Sam Hawgood.

Whether this incident was investigated by UCSF PD as a potential violation of Penal Code §422.6 (hate crime), reported by UCSF PD to the California Department of Justice as a hate crime, classified as a Clery Act hate crime in UCSF's Annual Security Report, or referred to the San Francisco Police Department for investigation is not documented in available public records.

Applicable Law: Penal Code §422.6 (hate crime — interference with civil rights); Penal Code §594 (vandalism). Clery Act (20 U.S.C. §1092(f)) — hate crimes occurring on campus must be classified and reported in the Annual Security Report.

Reported to POST/DOJ? UNKNOWN — Whether this incident was reported as a Clery Act hate crime and investigated as a Penal Code §422.6 violation is not documented in available public records.

Sources: House Energy and Commerce Committee letter, July 2024 — energycommerce.house.gov; UCSF Chancellor statements.
Spring 2024
GAZA SOLIDARITY ENCAMPMENT AT UCSF MEDICAL CENTER — AB 48 COMPLIANCE STATUS

During the spring 2024 nationwide campus encampment movement, a Gaza solidarity encampment was established in front of the UCSF medical center. The House Energy and Commerce Committee, in its July 2024 investigation letter, described the encampment as causing "significant disruption to campus and health care operations, as well as great distress for members of the community, including health care professionals and patients." Jewish healthcare professionals at UCSF expressed safety concerns, including fears that private information would be made public.

The specific arrest count and use-of-force details for UCSF's encampment response were not separately documented in available public sources. The September 12, 2025 LAist investigation — which examined all ten UC campus police departments' compliance with Assembly Bill 48 (AB 48) — specifically noted: "UC Merced and UCSF, to date, have not reported any use of less-lethal weapons."

Whether crowd-control weapons were deployed during UCSF's encampment response, and whether AB 48 reporting was therefore required and not filed, is not conclusively established in available public records. UCSF PD is not listed on the California Department of Justice's AB 48 reporting webpage as of September 12, 2025.

Of additional note: UCSF's encampment occurred at an active medical center. The hospital security mandates applicable to UCSF PD — specifically Health & Safety Code §§1257.7–1257.8 and Cal/OSHA §3342 — impose security and violence-prevention obligations at medical facilities that are not present at other UC campuses. Whether these obligations affected the UCSF PD's encampment response is not documented in available public records.

Applicable Law: AB 48 (PC §§13652, 13652.1) — requires reporting of any use of kinetic energy projectiles, chemical agents, or other crowd-control weapons during crowd management; First Amendment assembly rights; Clery Act campus safety reporting; HSC §§1257.7–1257.8 and Cal/OSHA §3342 (hospital security mandates).

Reported to POST/DOJ? UNKNOWN — UCSF PD is not listed on the DOJ AB 48 reporting webpage. Whether any incident requiring AB 48 reporting occurred is not independently confirmed.

Sources: House Energy and Commerce Committee letter, July 2024; LAist, September 12, 2025.
July 2024 – Present
ACTIVE FEDERAL CONGRESSIONAL INVESTIGATION — ANTISEMITISM AT UCSF — UCSF PD DOCUMENTS SPECIFICALLY SUBPOENAED

July 31, 2024 — Investigation Opened: The House Energy and Commerce Committee opened a formal investigation into antisemitic harassment and intimidation at UCSF and UCSF Health medical centers. A seven-page investigative letter was sent to UCSF Chancellor Sam Hawgood, signed by Committee Chair Cathy McMorris Rodgers, Health Subcommittee Chair Brett Guthrie, and Oversight Subcommittee Chair Morgan Griffith.

The committee letter cited the following specific documented incidents and conditions at UCSF: (1) A Gaza solidarity encampment at the UCSF medical center causing disruption to health care operations; (2) safety concerns expressed by Jewish healthcare professionals at UCSF, including fears that private information would be made public; (3) numerous antisemitic public statements by faculty, staff, and students at UCSF; (4) public statements claiming that patients should fear being treated by Jewish doctors; (5) the March 2024 defacement of the UCSF cancer center with antisemitic language invoking the Holocaust; (6) hundreds of complaints filed with UCSF's Office for the Prevention of Harassment and Discrimination (OPHD), with most complainants allegedly told the reported conduct was protected speech; and (7) UCSF assigning a counselor who had allegedly posted antisemitic remarks to support Jewish faculty who had reported antisemitism to the university.

The committee warned that federal funding — including Department of Health and Human Services grants and Medicare/Medicaid reimbursements — could be withheld if UCSF failed to address the documented conditions.

August 21, 2025 — Follow-Up Congressional Letter — UCSF PD Documents Specifically Requested: The House Education and Workforce Committee sent a follow-up letter to UCSF requesting documents by September 8, 2025. The document production request specifically included materials in the possession of the UCSF Police Department. This request placed UCSF PD under an active federal document production obligation.

Investigation Scope Expansion: The congressional investigation was subsequently expanded to include the Gladstone Institutes, a UCSF-affiliated research organization. By April 2025, the Paul Weiss law firm, retained in connection with the investigation, reportedly recommended the termination of a UCSF professor for conduct connected to the antisemitism investigation.

UCSF PD Direct Involvement: The federal investigation directly implicates UCSF PD in four documented respects: (1) The encampment at the UCSF medical center occurred within UCSF PD's primary patrol jurisdiction; (2) the August 2025 document production request specifically named the UCSF Police Department; (3) the physical security of Jewish healthcare workers at UCSF's medical facilities is a UCSF PD operational responsibility; and (4) the March 2024 cancer center defacement is a potential hate crime investigation within UCSF PD's investigative jurisdiction under Penal Code §422.6.

Applicable Law: Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 (42 U.S.C. §2000d); Clery Act hate crime reporting (20 U.S.C. §1092(f)); Penal Code §422.6 (hate crimes); HSC §§1257.7–1257.8 (hospital security obligations); federal document production obligations.

Status: UNDER ACTIVE FEDERAL INVESTIGATION — Congressional document production requests specifically name the UCSF Police Department. Investigation was ongoing as of the compilation date of this report (March 15, 2026).

Sources: House E&C Committee, July 2024; House Education & Workforce Committee, August 21, 2025; JNS, August 1, 2024; J. Weekly, April 25, 2025; Inside Higher Ed, June 4, 2024; UCSF Chancellor's Office; U.S. House Speaker — Antisemitism Report, December 2024.
