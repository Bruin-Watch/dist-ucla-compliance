# uc-police-system
Behave Like Heroes — Law Enforcement Without Enforcement.

This document presents facts as found in public records, legislative materials, academic sources, journalistic reporting, institutional documents, and the University of California’s own published and internal records — including audit reports, task force findings, Regents minutes, and departmental policies — together with the application of law, statute, rule, procedure, or other legal mechanism to those specific facts.

The comparative framework identifies structural differences between UC police departments and municipal agencies of comparable size, and applies the documented federal standard for pattern-or-practice intervention to the documented UC police record.

Source materials cited herein are matters of public record and are independently verifiable. Readers are encouraged to verify cited sources independently and to form their own assessments, opinions, and conclusions based on that independent review.

Nothing in this document should be construed as a legal conclusion, legal advice, or a finding of liability. The author presents this record for the purpose of public accountability and regulatory review.

This document is submitted to two audiences: the California Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training (POST), which has regulatory jurisdiction over all UC police departments; and any federal authority with jurisdiction under 34 U.S.C. § 12601.
