Section A
Department Overview
FieldDetail
Agency NameUniversity of California Berkeley Police Department (UCPD Berkeley)
EstablishedFormally established by UC Board of Regents in September 1947 under State Education Code Sections 20221–20222. Campus security presence dates to 1925 under Captain Walter J. Lee.
Legal AuthorityCalifornia Penal Code §830.2(b) (sworn peace officers); California Education Code §92600 (primary jurisdiction over UC-owned properties plus one-mile perimeter). Full statewide law enforcement authority.
POST StatusPOST-participating agency. All officers hold POST certification. Officers subject to SB 2 certification/decertification requirements effective January 1, 2023.
Sworn OfficersApproximately 49 sworn officers as of August 2025 (actively hiring).
Annual BudgetUC-wide police spending doubled from $75.3 million (2009–10) to $148.5 million (2019–20) per UAW 2865 Research Working Group (February 2021). Security spending for 2017 free speech events totaled $3,910,259 (Aug. 27–Sept. 27, 2017), with UCOP splitting costs 50/50 with the campus; normal annual allotment was $200,000. People's Park security 2022–2024 totaled $16.1 million. Sources: UAW 2865, Feb 2021; Berkeleyside, Feb 6, 2018; Daily Cal, Feb 4, 2018; Berkeley News, Feb 28, 2024.
Campus Population33,122 undergraduates and 13,029 graduate and professional students enrolled in Fall 2025, for a student enrollment total of 46,151 — before addition of faculty and staff. Source: UC Berkeley Quick Facts, Fall 2025.
JurisdictionUC Berkeley main campus plus one-mile perimeter in the City of Berkeley (population ~124,000). Includes People's Park (2.8-acre parcel listed on the National Register of Historic Places, May 24, 2022).
Current ChiefYogananda Pittman (February 1, 2023–present). Former Acting Chief of U.S. Capitol Police (January 8–July 23, 2021, following January 6 insurrection). B.S. Psychology (Morgan State), M.P.A. (Marist College), Ph.D. candidate (West Chester University). Currently managing simultaneous DOJ, FBI, and DOE investigations stemming from November 2025 TPUSA event.
IACLEA AccreditationUCPD Berkeley achieved IACLEA accreditation. Status: accredited.
Oversight BodyPolice Accountability Board (PAB), established 2023 under the UC Community Safety Plan. 10 members including 4 students. Reviews redacted investigation reports. Advisory recommendations only — Chief retains full and final authority on disposition. The PAB and the Chancellor's Independent Advisory Board (IAB) on Police Accountability and Community Safety together replaced the former Police Review Board (PRB), which was dissolved. Sources: UC Berkeley Chancellor's Office — PAB; UC Berkeley Chancellor's Office — IAB.
Union RepresentationFederated University Police Officers' Association (FUPOA).
Active Federal Investigations (as of Feb 2026)DOJ Civil Rights Division investigation: per AAG Harmeet K. Dhillon's public announcement of November 11, 2025, the investigation was initiated citing potential violations of 18 U.S.C. §§241 and 242 (conspiracy against rights; deprivation of rights under color of law) — no formal DOJ charging document or complaint confirmed as of report date. FBI San Francisco Field Office / JTTF investigation: digital tip line opened November 14, 2025 per media reports. DOE Clery Act focused review (2022–2025): officially confirmed, DOE press release November 25, 2025. Alameda County DA prosecution of Dphrepaulezz: ongoing. All related to November 10, 2025 TPUSA event and broader compliance history. Sources: SF Standard, Nov 11, 2025; ABC7, Nov 2025; DOE press release, Nov 25, 2025.

May 15, 1969 — People's Park "Bloody Thursday"
Pre-Mandatory Reporting Era — Included to Document Pattern of Conduct
CategoryDetail
Incident DescriptionPolice and National Guard troops (deployed by Governor Reagan) opened fire on protesters and bystanders during a confrontation over UC Berkeley's attempt to reclaim the People's Park lot. Included as foundational context for the institutional relationship between UCPD, People's Park, and use of force.

August 25, 1992 — Rosebud Denovo Fatal Shooting
Pre-Mandatory Reporting Era — Included to Document Pattern of Conduct
CategoryDetail
Incident DescriptionRosebud Denovo (née Laura Marie Miller), 19, a homeless anarchist activist and People's Park defender, broke into University House (Chancellor Chang-Lin Tien's residence) at 5:51 a.m. UCPD responded to the silent alarm and escorted Tien and his wife to safety. Oakland Police Department officers with canine units entered the house. OPD Officer Craig Chew shot and killed Denovo after she allegedly lunged at him with a machete. She was shot three times (autopsy showed at least one shot in the back). Alameda County DA determined the shooting was justified in October 1992. Chew had previously been investigated three times for excessive force at BPD before joining OPD. NOTE: Shot by OPD officer, not UCPD, but on UCPD-jurisdictional property during a UCPD-initiated response.
Applicable Law / Reporting RequirementPRE-MANDATORY REPORTING: Predates SB 1421, AB 48. OIS reporting to DOJ under then-applicable statutes. Government Code §12525 (predecessor reporting). Included to establish pattern of fatal force incidents on UCPD-jurisdictional property.
Reported to POST/DOJ?Pre-Mandatory — Alameda County DA reviewed and ruled justified (October 1992). Pre-SB 1421. Included to document pattern.

November 9, 2011 — Occupy Cal Baton Beatings
Pre-Mandatory Reporting Era — Included to Document Pattern of Conduct
CategoryDetail
Incident DescriptionApproximately 1,500 demonstrators gathered at Sproul Plaza for "Occupy Cal." After protesters erected seven tents and formed a human chain, dozens of police in riot gear — including UCPD officers and Alameda County Sheriff's deputies — pushed through the human chain using 36-inch riot batons, jabbing protesters in chests, stomachs, groins, and abdomens. Cal.) sought $15 million. 9th Circuit unanimously dismissed on qualified immunity (Felarca v. Birgeneau, 891 F.3d 809, 9th Cir. 2018). Pattern documented: a federal appellate judge stated on the record that force appeared excessive, yet zero financial accountability resulted due to qualified immunity.
Reported to POST/DOJ?Pre-Mandatory — Pre-AB 48. 9th Circuit Judge Watford stated baton-jabbing appeared to constitute excessive force. Zero accountability due to qualified immunity.

2009–2016 (Review Initiated 2014; FPRD September 2019; Settlement September 2020) — Clery Act Program Review and $2.35 Million Fine
CategoryDetail
Incident DescriptionCase Number: PRCN 201440328749. DOE initiated Clery Act Program Review of UC Berkeley in 2014, covering 2009–2016. On September 17, 2019, DOE issued Final Program Review Determination finding UC Berkeley failed to comply with the Clery Act. Settlement Agreement signed September 9, 2020 by Chancellor Carol T. Christ and Susan D. Crim (DOE). FINE: $2,350,000 — at the time, the third-largest Clery Act fine in history (behind Michigan State $4.5M and Penn State $2.4M; now fourth-largest after Liberty University $14M in 2024). The review examined approximately 32,000 records and found 1,125 MISCLASSIFIED INCIDENTS. Of these, 87% (982 incidents) were disciplinary referrals for liquor, drug, and weapons violations wrongly classified as campus policy violations rather than law violations. Additional findings: (1) Failure to issue timely warnings for 30 incidents during 2014–2016; February 2017 Milo protest did not receive emergency notification for nearly an hour after violence escalated; five burglaries over two months received no timely warning until after the fifth incident; two sexual assaults received no timely warning until four days after being reported. (2) Three hate crimes not reported — one verbal intimidation via phone (race), one written intimidation via email (race), one vandalism (sexual orientation). (3) Unable to provide evidence that 2010, 2011, and 2012 Annual Security Reports were distributed before October 1 deadline; 2013 ASR had broken website link. (4) Twelve Daily Crime Log entries during 2010–2013 incorrectly classified or missing required elements. (5) DOE found UC Berkeley "lacks administrative capacity" for Clery compliance. Corrective actions: training all CSAs, revised policies, updated crime statistics, two-year post-settlement monitoring period with on-site visits.
Applicable Law / Reporting RequirementClery Act (20 U.S.C. §1092(f)); Higher Education Act §485(f); 34 C.F.R. §668.46. This is a DOCUMENTED FEDERAL FINDING of systemic reporting failures spanning at least seven years.
Reported to POST/DOJ?Not Compliant — DOE found 1,125 misclassified incidents, 30 timely warning failures, 3 unreported hate crimes, and ASR distribution failures. $2,350,000 fine imposed.
Source(s) Federal Student Aid settlement — https://studentaid.gov/sites/default/files/university-of-california-berkely-settlement-090920.pdf
Berkeley News, Sept 10, 2020 — https://news.berkeley.edu/2020/09/10/uc-berkeleys-clery-act-compliance-review-ending-after-6-years/

November 10, 2025 — TPUSA Event — Violence, Active DOJ/FBI/DOE Investigations
Status as of February 2026: DOJ Civil Rights Division investigation, FBI JTTF investigation, DOE Clery Act focused review (2022–2025), UCPD internal investigation, and Alameda County DA prosecution all remain ACTIVE and ongoing.
CategoryDetail
Incident DescriptionTurning Point USA memorial event for Charlie Kirk at Zellerbach Hall. Approximately 300+ law enforcement officers from UCPD, Berkeley PD, San Francisco Sheriff's Office, Alameda County Sheriff's Office, CHP, and private security firms were deployed. Officers wore riot gear and carried PepperBall launchers. A 45-year-old male attendee was struck in the head with a glass bottle or jar thrown from the crowd — suffered head laceration, transported to Highland Hospital. Jihad Dphrepaulezz, 25, of Oakland, arrested for felony robbery and battery. Protesters threw paint-filled glass bottles, rocks, and tear gas canisters at officers and bystanders. Four UC Berkeley students (ages 20–22) arrested at approximately midnight on felony vandalism charges. Two additional arrests (Christopher Joseph Benton, 48; Jay Eduardo Maytorena, 22). Total: approximately 8 arrests. On November 11, AAG Harmeet K. Dhillon announced DOJ Civil Rights Division investigation (18 U.S.C. §§241, 242 — conspiracy to deprive/deprivation of rights under color of law). FBI San Francisco Field Office launched digital tip line November 14. On November 25, DOE initiated Clery Act focused review (2022–2025), explicitly citing prior $2.35M Clery fine. Secretary McMahon stated UC Berkeley "allowed a protest...to turn unruly and violent, jeopardizing the safety of its students and staff." DOE requested 11 categories of records with 30-day deadline.
Applicable Law / Reporting RequirementClery Act §485(f) (timely warning/emergency notification — DOE review explicitly examining this); AB 48 (PC §§13652, 13652.1 — if PepperBall launchers or other crowd-control weapons were deployed); POST use-of-force reporting; 18 U.S.C. §§241, 242 (federal civil rights); 2018 BCR/YAF settlement (DOJ investigating whether UC Berkeley violated terms).
Reported to POST/DOJ?Under Active Federal Investigation — DOJ, FBI, DOE all investigating. UCPD not listed on DOJ AB 48 reporting page. Whether PepperBall launchers were actually fired (vs. carried) has not been confirmed in available sources. Clery Act compliance for this event is the specific subject of the November 25, 2025 DOE focused review.

AB 48
PC §§13652, 13652.1
Effective Jan 1, 2022		 Non-Compliant UCPD Berkeley was not listed on the California DOJ's statewide AB 48 reporting webpage as of the September 12, 2025 LAist investigation. AB 48 requires every law enforcement agency to document and publicly report any use of kinetic energy projectiles and chemical agents used during crowd control situations, submitted to CA DOJ within 60 days. Eight of ten UC campus police departments share this non-compliant status. LAist, September 12, 2025 — https://laist.com/news/criminal-justice/uc-campus-police-protest-reporting-law
