Legal Significance — Chain of Custody, Admissibility, and Spoliation

The seven classifications above were issued in writing by sworn California peace officers — UCLA Police Department personnel, including Capt. Jeff Chobanian (Badge #307), Lt. James Echols, Lt. Richard L. Davis, and Chief of Police Craig Valenzuela in his capacity as the final decisionmaker on these matters — speaking as state actors with custodial authority over Harold’s UCLA PD personnel file. The legal weight of those statements, in any forum that adjudicates Harold’s classification, is governed by California’s evidentiary framework:

• California Evidence Code § 1280 (Public Employee Records Exception). Records made by a public employee within the scope of duty are admissible to prove the act or condition stated. Capt. Chobanian’s August 25, 2023 letter, his February 10, 2026 email, and Lt. Echols’s February 24, 2026 email were issued on UCLA PD letterhead by sworn personnel acting in the scope of their administrative duties. They are admissible as substantive evidence of UCLA PD’s official position on Harold’s status — against the agency that issued them.

• California Evidence Code § 1271 (Business Records Exception). UCLA PD’s internal personnel file — the source Capt. Chobanian and Lt. Echols expressly cited — is itself admissible business-record evidence. Chobanian’s direct quotation from that file (the “medically separated on March 1, 1996” designation, classification #4 above) carries the file’s own evidentiary weight as a matter of law.

• California Evidence Code § 413 (Failure to Produce Evidence). UCLA PD’s documented inability to locate Harold’s archived personnel file (Lt. Echols, February 24, 2026, 3:04 PM, classification #7 above) permits the trier of fact to draw the inference that the missing file would, if produced, support Harold’s position rather than UC’s.

• Spoliation doctrine. Under Cedars-Sinai Medical Center v. Superior Court (1998) 18 Cal.4th 1, and its progeny, where a party with custodial duty over evidence loses, destroys, or fails to produce that evidence, the trier of fact may presume the lost evidence was unfavorable to the custodian. UCLA PD, as the custodial agency for Harold’s sworn-officer personnel file, bears that risk of presumption on every classification question now in dispute.

In any forum that adjudicates Harold’s classification — the Workers’ Compensation Appeals Board, an administrative review, or a court of competent jurisdiction — Capt. Chobanian’s and Lt. Echols’s emails are not opinion, hearsay, or one party’s side of the story. They are the official position of UCLA PD, admissible on UC’s own records, against UC.

UCLA PD, by the hand of its own captain, citing the very case that controls the question, classified Harold as medically separated due to industrial injury — which under Jacobs and the Gold Book is the precise factual predicate for the legal classification “Duty Disability Income recipient.” The agency with custodial authority over the underlying personnel record has therefore confirmed Harold’s UCRP status, by name, on UCLA PD letterhead, in writing, citing the same case law that binds UC.
