Section C: Records Integrity and Data Breach Questions (Q17–Q25)

Question 17
Ida Fong / Max Harold, February–March 2021

Documents: On February 24, 2021, UC Benefits employee Ida Fong called Harold regarding Max Harold being listed as “disabled” in UC’s system. Max Harold was never disabled. Harold IS the family member with the documented permanent disability determination. On March 18, 2021, Fong called again from (510) 987-0665, duration 16 minutes 3 seconds. Fong processed a premium refund for Max’s overpaid premiums. Fong stated records were “lost during computer system upgrades.” Harold’s March 12, 2021 email to Fong memorializes these events contemporaneously. UC issued the refund. Max was removed from the 1095-B by 2022.

(a) UC corrected Max Harold’s records (Max was never disabled), issued a premium refund, and removed Max from the 1095-B — all within approximately one year. Why did UC not simultaneously restore Harold’s disability classification, given the documented Octagon permanent disability determination (August 8, 2001) that has never been revoked?

(b) Which specific computer system upgrade caused the record loss? What specific records were lost? What steps were taken to recover or reconstruct them?

Source Files: EX 15 — Ida Fong Email Thread (March 12, 2021) — combined PDF: pp. 1–6 email to Ida Fong; pp. 7–11 Harold to Fong Gmail thread re UC Health Insurance Payments; pp. 12–13 Octagon letter attachment
Question 18
The Contemporaneous Record — March 12, 2021 Email

Document: Harold’s March 12, 2021 email to Ida Fong is timestamped and contemporaneous. UC acted on this email by issuing a premium refund for Max Harold and by removing Max from subsequent 1095-B forms. These are contemporaneous, timestamped, UC-acted-upon documents — not retroactive fabrications.

Does the Plan Administrator accept the March 12, 2021 email and UC’s subsequent refund issuance as evidence that (a) UC’s disability coding system was corrupted (the wrong family member was coded as disabled), and (b) UC had contemporaneous notice that Harold’s disability classification required correction?

Source Files: EX 15 — Ida Fong Email Thread (March 12, 2021), pp. 7–11 (Harold to Fong Gmail thread, contemporaneous record of premium refund and 1095-B correction)
Question 19
UC Corrected Max But Not Harold

Documents: UC corrected Max Harold’s erroneous disability coding, issued a premium refund, and removed Max from the 1095-B — all within approximately one year of discovering the error. Despite the same February 24, 2021 conversation establishing that the disability coding had been applied to the wrong family member, Harold’s own disability classification has never been restored in over five years.

Please explain this disparity.

Source Files: Same as Q17 and Q18 above  |  2022_1095-B.pdf (Max removed)
Question 20
UCLA CFO Stephen Agostini — Failed System Upgrade and Erroneous Records

Documents: In a February 6, 2026 interview published by the Daily Bruin on February 13, 2026, UCLA Vice Chancellor and Chief Financial Officer Stephen Agostini stated that UCLA’s annual financial reports posted on its website since 2002 are “erroneous and unaudited.” He identified a $425 million structural deficit resulting from “financial management flaws and failures.” He stated the Ascend Finance Transformation Project — UCLA’s initiative to replace its legacy mainframe financial system designed in the 1980s with Oracle Cloud — cost at least $213 million over seven years, including $150 million with “nothing to show for it.” Chancellor Julio Frenk fired Agostini on February 17, 2026 — four days after publication.

(a) The “since 2002” window identified by UCLA’s own CFO encompasses the entire period of Harold’s records disputes: the 2003 workers’ compensation settlement, the 2014–2015 reclassification from disability to retirement income, the initiation of premium deductions, and the disappearance of workers’ compensation settlement records. What steps, if any, has the Plan Administrator taken to determine whether Harold’s records were affected by the system failures UCLA’s CFO publicly confirmed?

(b) Is the legacy mainframe system from the 1980s that Agostini described the same system that stored Harold’s 1996 disability classification and 2003 settlement records?

(c) Provide documentation of any investigation into the impact of these confirmed system failures on Harold’s records.

Source Files: EX 30 Stephen Agostini's Public Financial Leadership at UCLA.pdf  |  Daily Bruin, February 13, 2026  |  Daily Bruin, February 17, 2026
Question 21
Data Breaches — Individual Impact Assessment Required

Documents: For EACH of the following documented data breaches, please state separately: (1) Was Harold notified as required by law? (2) Was any investigation conducted regarding the impact on Harold’s specific records? (3) If so, provide the results. (4) If not, state that for the record.

(a) Breach #7: Anthem (February 18, 2014 – January 27, 2015 discovery). 78.8 million records compromised. Compromised data explicitly included “employment information and income data.” Harold’s disability-to-retirement reclassification occurred during this exact breach window.

(b) Breach #8: UCLA Health System (July 2014 – May 2015). 4.5 million patient records breached. Overlaps directly with Harold’s reclassification period.

(c) Breach #12: UC Accellion (December 24, 2020). 547,000+ UC individuals’ data compromised. Compromised data explicitly included disability accommodation information — precisely the category of data at issue in Harold’s case. The same year Harold’s account number first changed.

(d) Breaches #13–19: Seven Separate Anthem Breaches (January 12, January 21, May 1, June 30, and additional dates in 2021). Occurring during the same period UC employee Ida Fong admitted records were “lost during computer system upgrades” and discovered the disability coding error.

Identify what portions of Harold’s UC file were compromised, corrupted, or modified during any documented breach.

Source Files: EX 23 UC 2020 NOTICE OF DATA BREACH.pdf  |  Breach documentation compiled from California Attorney General Data Breach Database and public records
Question 22
Anthem Blue Cross Portal — Coverage History

Documents: Harold’s Anthem Blue Cross member portal (Member ID U5X808M87210) displays only three plan periods: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024; January 1, 2025 – December 31, 2025; and January 1, 2026 – Present. No records exist prior to January 1, 2024 despite 20+ years of continuous coverage and premium payments. Additionally, $14,094.36 in paid claims were documented on the portal and subsequently erased. The December 21, 2023 – January 4, 2024 inpatient hospitalization for Valley Fever (Coccidioidomycosis) is completely absent from all Anthem portal records.

(a) Reconcile the Anthem portal showing records only from January 1, 2024 with UC’s own 1095-B forms confirming continuous coverage from at least 2016 through 2025.

(b) $14,094.36 in documented paid claims were erased from the portal. What happened to these claims? Who authorized their removal?

(c) Harold’s December 21, 2023 – January 4, 2024 inpatient hospitalization for Valley Fever is completely absent from the insurer’s records. Please explain how a 14-day inpatient hospitalization can be absent from the insurer’s portal.

Source Files: Anthem Blue Cross portal screenshots (Harold’s possession)  |  1095-B forms: 2016_1095-B.pdf through 2023_1095-B.pdf

(a) Why did Harold receive zero notifications from Anthem while his wife received one notification at the same Arizona address?

(b) Produce the 2025 Summary of Benefits and Coverage or explain its absence from the portal.
