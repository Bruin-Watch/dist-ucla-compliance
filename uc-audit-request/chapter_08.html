Section D: Institutional Accountability (Q26–Q33)

Question 26
Bustamante Unanswered Audit Request — November 9, 2021

Documents: On November 9, 2021, attorney Edgar A. Saenz, Esq. submitted a formal “Request for Audit of Charles A. Harold, Jr. files” addressed to Alexander Bustamante, Senior Vice President and Chief Compliance & Audit Officer, University of California. The letter was sent by certified mail and email to [email protected]. Gmail delivery was confirmed November 9, 2021 at 3:10 PM. The letter specifically requested: (1) an audit of Harold’s workers’ compensation and disability income case to determine his status; (2) an audit to determine what Personally Identifiable Information belonging to Harold was breached during UC data breaches; (3) an audit of Harold’s internal UC records to determine what records were lost during internal computer system upgrades; and (4) reversal of UC’s 2015 reclassification of Harold’s disability income to service retirement. As of April 14, 2026, that request has remained unanswered for over four years and five months.

(a) Please explain why UC’s Chief Compliance & Audit Officer did not respond to a formal audit request from an attorney of record regarding a documented whistleblower’s disability and benefits records.

(b) Has the Plan Administrator consulted with Mr. Bustamante’s office in connection with the current review? If so, what records did that office provide? If not, how does the Plan Administrator intend to make a determination without doing so?

Question 27
Williams-Cook Full Timeline

Documents: The complete documented timeline of Williams-Cook’s 28-day assignment:

January 2026 (during the month): UC internally stopped deducting the $727.48 monthly premium from Harold’s pension. Unify Financial Credit Union records confirm the February 1, 2026 deposit increased by exactly $727.48.

January 29, 2026: Harold filed “Immediate Hold Request on Retiree Medical Coverage Changes” with RASC, notifying RASC of his upcoming February 25, 2026 cancer surgery.

January 30, 2026: Williams-Cook was assigned to Harold’s case.

February 11, 2026: Williams-Cook called Harold. Harold informed her of the evidence supporting his position. She said she would call back after his surgery. She never called back.

February 25, 2026: Harold’s radical prostatectomy. RASC had formal notice of this surgery date.

February 27, 2026: Williams-Cook DATED her denial letter — two days after Harold’s cancer surgery, while he was in active post-surgical recovery. She used the superseded UBEN 100 form (R10/21). She left the last digit off her phone number (800-888-826 instead of 800-888-8267). She never requested any of Harold’s supporting documents. She never addressed any of Harold’s six prior RASC filings.

March 29, 2026: Harold RECEIVED the letter via U.S. mail — 30 days after the date on the letter — per envelope postmark.

(a) What is the total number of communications Williams-Cook initiated with Harold during her 28-day assignment?

(b) Was Williams-Cook aware of Harold’s February 25, 2026 surgery when she dated the determination February 27, 2026?

(c) Explain the 30-day gap between the date on the determination (February 27, 2026) and the date Harold received it (March 29, 2026, per envelope postmark).

(d) Has the incomplete phone number (800-888-826) been corrected?

(e) What specific records did Williams-Cook review? List each document by name.

(f) UC executed the coverage cancellation in January 2026 — BEFORE Williams-Cook was assigned on January 30, 2026. How was her review genuine when the outcome had already been implemented?

Source Files: Williams-Cook Determination with postmarked envelope (Harold’s possession)  |  EX 35 2026-01-29 Immediate Hold Request on Retiree Medical Coverage Changes.pdf [Forthcoming]
Question 28
Workers’ Compensation Settlement, March 2003

Documents: Workers’ Compensation Claim No. 199650445, Date of Injury February 16, 1996. Settled March 2003. Settlement terms include lifetime health coverage and a CCW permit. Octagon Risk Services permanent disability determination dated August 8, 2001 has never been revoked. California Labor Code § 5804 provides that the WCAB retains continuing jurisdiction over settlements. No WCAB modification order revoking or modifying the settlement health coverage provisions is on record.

(a) Has the Plan Administrator reviewed the 2003 settlement terms? If so, reconcile the Via Benefits transfer with the lifetime health coverage provisions of the settlement.

(b) If the settlement file has not been reviewed, how does the Plan Administrator intend to make a determination without reviewing it?

(c) Identify any WCAB order modifying or terminating the settlement health coverage provisions. If none exists, state that for the record.
