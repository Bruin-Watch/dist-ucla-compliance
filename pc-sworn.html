I. Sworn Statement

UC Whistleblower Protection Policy 1100563 § B (Definitions)
“Sworn Statement — A statement signed by the Complainant and made under penalty of perjury that states that the contents of the complaint are true or are believed by the Complainant to be true. A complaint submitted without a Sworn Statement will not be processed under this policy.” — UC Policy 1100563
Sworn Statement of Charles A. Harold Jr.

I, Charles A. Harold Jr., hereby declare:

1. I am the Complainant in the foregoing Sworn Retaliation Complaint and Protected Disclosure filed pursuant to California Government Code § 8547.10 and the University of California Whistleblower Protection Policy 1100563.

2. The following facts bearing on my employment status and classification are true and correct to the best of my knowledge and belief, and are supported by the documentary record incorporated by reference herein:

  • February 16, 1996 — Sustained an on-duty industrial injury at the UCLA Police Department. Workers’ Compensation Claim No. 199650445 filed. Date of Injury: February 16, 1996.
  • March 1, 1996 — UCLA PD, under Acting Chief Karl T. Ross (named defendant, SC022125), classified me as “medically separated.” No DDI payments received from UC for approximately 84 months. Ross subsequently entered a “resigned” classification into the UC system, suppressing DDI payments; that entry is documented as the first of fifteen contradictory status classifications issued by UC over thirty years.
  • August 8, 2001 — Octagon Risk Services issued a permanent and stationary determination: 6¾% permanent disability, $3,075.98/month DDI, Workers’ Compensation Claim No. 199650445. This determination has never been revoked, modified, or superseded by any medical authority or by the Workers’ Compensation Appeals Board. No WCAB modification order has ever issued.
  • September 6, 2002 — UCLA PD issued CCW Permit No. 1, signed by Chief Clarence Chapman. Card states “RETIRED.” First institutional acknowledgment of retired status — six years and six months after medical separation, four years after dismissal of SC022125 in which Ross was a named defendant.
  • March 2003 — Workers’ compensation case settled. Settlement terms: lifetime paid health insurance for myself and my family; DDI of $3,075.98/month tax-free; right to carry a concealed weapon as a retired peace officer. Full DDI payments commenced.
  • September 29, 2009 – June 8, 2010 — Thomas Herz, UC Office of the President employee, corresponded with me regarding my disability status. His departmental title changed three times during this single correspondence thread: “Retiree Insurance” (September 2009), “Health & Welfare Administration” (January 2010), and “Retiree Administration Service Center” (March 2010) — all at telephone number 510-987-0229, the same UCOP exchange used by Erin Morse (510-987-0762) who issued the April 9, 2026 RASC 90-Day Review. On March 29, 2010, Herz confirmed my classification in writing: “The requirement does apply to you as it does to all other retirees on disability.” I informed Herz in writing on March 28, 2010 that my settlement included lifetime medical coverage and asked him to verify with UC legal counsel before taking any action. He stated he was “not aware of any settlements” and did not consult UC legal as I requested. On April 6, 2010, Herz threatened that failure to provide an SSA determination “can result in an offset fee being imposed on your check and termination of UC medical coverage.” On April 9, 2010, SSA confirmed I was ineligible for SSDI because my income exceeded the Substantial Gainful Activity threshold. The SSDI avenue failed. The same RASC office — same telephone exchange, different name, different employee — attempted the identical outcome in 2025–2026 through the Via Benefits transfer pathway.
  • August 29, 2013 — UCLA PD issued CCW Permit No. 2, signed by Chief James D. Herren. Card states: “RETIRED. Retired in good standing from service as a Peace Officer of the state of California.” Badge #341, Rank: Officer, Date Retired: 03/01/96, Service Years: 8. Issued pursuant to California Penal Code §§ 25455, 25460 & 25465. Expires: August 29, 2018.
  • 2002 – 2020 — UCLA PD maintained me on its active retiree CCW qualification list continuously for approximately 18 years, sending range qualification notices in 2011, 2014, 2015, 2016, and 2017.
  • Tax Years 2010 – 2014 — UC issued annual IRS Forms 1099-R under Distribution Code 3 (Disability), confirming my income was tax-free Duty Disability Income, filed by UC under penalty of perjury:

No WCAB order authorized this conversion. No notice was provided to me. UC simultaneously began deducting $727.48 per month in health insurance premiums from my retirement payments — premiums that were employer-paid under the 2003 workers’ compensation settlement.
  • 2015 (ongoing) — UC unilaterally converted my income classification from DDI (Code 3, 92.7% tax-free) to service retirement (Code 2, then Code 7, 100% taxable). The Workers’ Compensation Appeals Board was never petitioned and never ruled on this conversion. No WCAB modification order has issued. The conversion was not authorized under California Labor Code § 5804. From 2016 forward, UC issued annual 1099-R forms under Code 2 (2016–2019) then Code 7 (2020–2025), 100% taxable, each filed by UC under penalty of perjury.
  • August 29, 2018 — CCW Permit No. 2 expired. UCLA PD sent no renewal notice and no range qualification communications of any kind.
  • March 2019 — After eighteen months of silence I initiated contact myself. UCLA PD administrative staff identified that my email address had been dropped from the retiree distribution list and corrected it. Sergeant James Echols confirmed my address and resumed communications.
  • March 23, 2020 — Sergeant Echols cancelled all range training due to COVID-19 and promised in writing to notify me promptly when training resumed. No such notification was ever sent. My correct, confirmed, operational email address remained in the system.
  • February and March 2021 — UC Benefits employee Ida Fong stated that records had been lost during computer system upgrades. UC identified my son Max Harold — who has no disability — as the disabled family member in UC’s records, issued a premium refund upon correction, and did not simultaneously correct my own disability classification.
  • November 9, 2021 — My attorney Edgar A. Saenz, Esq., submitted a formal audit request by certified mail to Alexander Bustamante, Senior Vice President and Chief Compliance & Audit Officer, University of California, requesting audit of my workers’ compensation, disability income, and personnel records and reversal of the 2015 conversion. No response has ever been received.
  • August 25, 2023 — Captain Jeff Chobanian (Badge #307) denied my CCW, stating: “After a comprehensive review of your file, I have determined you are ineligible for a CCW. Your status was designated as medically separated on March 1, 1996.” This contradicted his August 21, 2023 address of me as an “active retiree” and contradicted 21 years of CCW issuance, renewal, and range qualification by UCLA PD treating me as a retiree.
  • January 1, 2026 — UC’s own systems automatically enrolled me in UC High Option Supplement to Medicare — a federally designated Medigap (Medicare Supplement) plan — confirmed simultaneously by UCRAYS (Medicare Coordination: Yes, $727.48/month, 100% UC contribution), Anthem Blue Cross portal (active coverage January 1, 2026), and Medicare.gov (Blue Cross of California listed as “Other Insurance,” the federal CMS designation for a Medigap policy, coverage start 1/1/2026). UC High Option Supplement to Medicare is available only to qualifying UC retirees and safety members — UC’s own automatic enrollment of the Complainant in this plan constitutes an institutional acknowledgment of qualifying status.
  • February 2, 2026 — Joshua Lewis, Via Benefits / Willis Towers Watson program administrator (1-866-322-2824), called me at 12:11 PM Arizona time (7 minutes 6 seconds) and confirmed I am ineligible for Via Benefits because of my disability rating, stated he would notify UC in writing that my application would not be accepted, and upon learning of my February 25, 2026 cancer surgery stated: “We need to fix this before then.”
  • February 9, 2026 — I served Captain Chobanian a written litigation hold and POBR records demand (EX 20) requiring production of eight categories of records by February 18, 2026.
  • February 10, 2026 — Captain Chobanian refused all further communication. The February 18, 2026 deadline lapsed. No records were produced.
  • February 24, 2026 — Lieutenant James Echols issued three irreconcilable custody-of-file statements in forty-one minutes: file “located” at 1:24 PM; file “located” again at 2:23 PM; file “archived and being searched for” at 3:04 PM. Captain Chobanian was CC’d on each.
  • February 27, 2026 — Beverly Jeanine Williams-Cook, Benefits Analyst III, UC RASC, dated a written Administrative Review Determination denying my re-enrollment two days after my radical prostatectomy, using the superseded UBEN 100 (R10/21) form, without requesting a single document from me, without addressing any of six prior RASC filings, with an incomplete phone number (800-888-826, missing the final digit), mailed late and received March 28–29, 2026 — 30 days after the date on the letter.
  • April 1, 2026 — Fifty-one days after the litigation hold, Lieutenant Echols stated the agency had received the wrong box and that my files, “if they still exist,” are difficult to locate. Captain Chobanian had confirmed reviewing the same file in writing on August 25, 2023 and February 10, 2026. These four statements cannot all be true on the same set of facts.
  • April 17, 2026 — The Definitive Institutional Admission. UCLA Compliance closed CMP373 finding that Captain Chobanian and Chief Valenzuela committed no policy violation in denying my CCW. The CCW denial was premised expressly on my “medically separated” status and current receipt of Duty Disability Income under UC Gold Book GO 21-02 § 1700.1(b)(1). By upholding that denial, UCLA Compliance — the highest UC compliance authority at the campus level — rendered an institutional determination that I am a DDI recipient who has not retired or separated from the University of California. If I were a regular retiree as UC RASC has simultaneously classified me since 2015, the CCW denial would have been wrongful and CMP373 would have been sustained in my favor. It was not. UCLA Compliance affirmed the DDI classification. That determination is UC’s own. It entitles me to: restoration of DDI tax status; employer-paid medical premiums under the 2003 WC settlement; removal from the Via Benefits transfer pathway; and re-enrollment in UC High Option Supplement to Medicare as a DDI recipient with no geographic restriction.

The University of California and its agents have issued fifteen mutually inconsistent written classifications of my employment status over thirty years. No two fully agree. No WCAB modification order has authorized any transition between them. The complete classification record is documented in the Bruin Watch evidentiary record at CH_06, chapter_05, chapter_07, and CH_13.

3. I have personal knowledge of the facts stated in this Sworn Retaliation Complaint and the documents and exhibits incorporated by reference herein, except as to those matters stated on information and belief, and as to those matters, I believe them to be true.
