Master Evidence Index and Court Records
Formal Request for Audit and Records Reconciliation

This evidentiary record constitutes a formal demand for audit pursuant to California Government Code § 8546.7, which provides that every contract involving expenditure of public funds exceeding $10,000 “shall be subject to the examination and audit of the California State Auditor.” The Legislature specifically intended this provision to apply to the Regents of the University of California. The contracts at issue — including UC’s systemwide agreements with Anthem Blue Cross, Accolade, and Via Benefits (Willis Towers Watson) — each involve public fund expenditures far exceeding this threshold.

The University of California constitutes a “public trust” under California Constitution, Article IX, Section 9. As a self-insured employer, UC is bound by Cal. Code Regs. Title 8, § 15400.2: claim files where future benefits may be payable “shall not” be destroyed.

A prior formal audit request was submitted November 9, 2021, to Chief Compliance & Audit Officer Alexander Bustamante. UC has never responded. This is the second such demand.

CASE 3: CCW MANDATE ACTION — Jacobs et al. v. The Regents of the University of California
Case Number B268758 (Court of Appeal); BS147764 (Los Angeles County Superior Court)
Court Court of Appeal of the State of California, Second Appellate District, Division Two
Filed / Published Opinion filed May 30, 2017; Certified for Publication June 27, 2017
Judge (Trial Court) Robert H. O’Brien, Superior Court of Los Angeles County
Appellate Panel Ashmann-Gerst, Acting P.J.; Chavez, J.; Goodman, J. (Ret.)
Plaintiffs/Appellants Allison Jacobs (UC Berkeley PD, injured on duty 2010, DDI effective 04/13/2013, medically separated 07/18/2013); Dennis Mueller (UC Santa Barbara PD, injured on duty 1997, DDI effective 07/02/1998, received CCW 1998–2013 then denied renewal); Federated University Police Officers Association (FUPOA) (250+ members, exclusive bargaining representative for nonsupervisory UC peace officers).
Defendant/Respondent The Regents of the University of California
Appellants’ Counsel Castillo Harper; Michael A. Morguess
Respondent’s Counsel Jones & Mayer; Martin J. Mayer, James R. Touchstone, Krista MacNevin Jee
Legal Question Whether disabled members under the UCRP who receive “Duty Disability Income” (DDI) are considered “retired” for purposes of entitlement to a retired identification card and concealed weapons endorsement under Penal Code §16690.
Holding Affirmed trial court denial. DDI recipients are NOT “retired” under the UCRP. The UCRP has no category of “disability retirement.” Under the UCRP, Disabled Members: (1) are not retired; (2) do not receive Retirement Income; and (3) can elect to retire when eligible but are never required to do so.
Key Regents Admission The Regents acknowledged that “its prior policy of issuing retired identification cards and endorsements for concealed weapons to Disabled Members receiving DDI was erroneous, and was properly discontinued.” This admission confirms that UC previously treated DDI recipients as retired for CCW purposes — exactly as Harold was treated from 2002–2017.
Critical FUPOA Connection FUPOA, which Harold founded, was a named plaintiff in this action. The case directly addresses the status contradiction that is central to Harold’s current claims. The court’s distinction between “disabled” and “retired” under UCRP supports Harold’s argument that UC cannot simultaneously treat him as retired (for tax purposes) and not retired (for CCW purposes).
Trial Court Finding re Jacobs Jacobs’s employment records describe the action taken as “Terminat[io]n” with “Reason” specified as “Medical Separation.” Mueller’s employment records “no longer exist.”
Significance to Current Case Establishes binding legal framework for DDI vs. retirement distinction under UCRP. UC’s admission of prior “erroneous” CCW issuance directly parallels Harold’s experience. Mueller’s lost employment records parallel Harold’s lost settlement records. The “Schödinger’s Retiree” contradiction (CH 9) flows directly from this legal framework.
